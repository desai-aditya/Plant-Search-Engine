North American natives. The various plants described here are upright or arching, typically 26 feet high and wide, often spreading by root suckers. Most are best used as informal hedges or as wild thickets for erosion control on steep banks. Clusters of small pink or white flowers in spring or early summer. Attractive round, berrylike fruit remains on stems after leaves drop in autumn; looks nice in winter arrangements, attracts birds. Plants are not usually damaged by browsing deer.
