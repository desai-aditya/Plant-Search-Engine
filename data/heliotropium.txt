Grown mainly for their fragrant summer flowers, which are individually tiny but borne in striking clusters 34 inches across. Attract both butterflies and hummingbirds. Although most often treated as annuals, they can be grown as short-lived perennials in the Tropical South. Plants can be massed in flower beds; they also look great in window boxes and planters. Provide well-drained soil.In Southern gardens, common heliotrope grows best with morning sun and afternoon shade. Pinch young plants to encourage bushy growth. If growing as a perennial, prune back hard in early spring. Be sure to keep watered during dry spells.
