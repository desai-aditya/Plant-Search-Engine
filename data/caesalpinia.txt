Ferny-leafed plants grown for their branch-end clusters of colorful blossoms featuring (except Caesalpinia platyloba) protruding stamens. Flowers attract hummingbirds. Plants grow quickly and easily in hot, sunny locations if given light, well-drained soil and infrequent, deep water- ing. Prune before first flush of spring growth to remove any dead or damaged wood and wayward branches; remove  lower limbs for treelike shape in shrubby species.Plants freeze to ground in colder part of range but rebound quickly in spring. In milder climates,  you can cut them to the ground in early spring to make a more compact shrub. 'Cream Puff' has cream-colored flowers and chartreuse leaves; 'Phoenix Bird' bears bright yellow blooms.
